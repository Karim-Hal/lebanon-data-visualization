Act 12011 – 2024

The Macro Shock

GDP collapsed by over 60%, inflation peaked above 200%, and the official exchange rate diverged sharply from the black market. Lebanon's trajectory compared against five regional peers.

GDP: Lebanon
Lebanon GDP in USD billions by year. Event markers show the 2019 banking crisis, 2020 Beirut port explosion, and 2021 subsidy removal. Source: World Bank WDI.
Official vs Unofficial Exchange Rate: The "Two Lebanons"
Y axis is logarithmic so the gap is readable across the full range. The shaded gap between the two lines is the crisis. At its peak, market traders charged ~50x the official rate.
Personal Remittances: % of GDP
In 2023, remittances equalled 33.3% of Lebanon's GDP — among the highest in the world. The diaspora is what kept Lebanon from total collapse.
Inflation Heatmap: Country × Year
Each cell shows the annual inflation rate from 2015 onwards. A diverging scale anchors at 0%: green = deflation, near-white = stable, orange-to-red = high inflation (capped at 300% so Lebanon’s 221% peak reads clearly). Hover for exact values.
GDP per Capita: Country Comparison
Annual GDP per capita in USD, 2015 onwards. Lebanon's collapse from ~$8k to under $4k stands out against peer trajectories. Country colours follow the project palette and stay consistent across all charts. Source: World Bank WDI · NY.GDP.PCAP.CD.
